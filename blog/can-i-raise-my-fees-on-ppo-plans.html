How to Identify and Raise Your PPO Fee Schedules

6 min read Updated January 2024

Insurance companies play a strategic game with dentists: they never disclose the maximum fees they'll reimburse, hoping you'll submit fees lower than their allowable amounts. By understanding this hidden fee structure and strategically raising your rates, you can capture thousands in revenue currently leaving your practice.

The Insurance Company Fee Game

One of the least transparent aspects of PPO agreements is the maximum fee schedule. Insurance companies will not tell you their absolute ceiling on what they'll pay for specific procedures. Instead, they hope your submitted fee schedule stays below their maximum, allowing them to retain profit margin.

When you submit your initial fees, the insurance company silently calculates whether you're below, at, or above their threshold. If you're too high, they reject your schedule. But here's what they won't tell you: your fees are likely well below their actual maximum allowable.

Understanding the Dynamic

Insurance companies employ what might be called a "silent rejection" strategy. They evaluate your fee schedule but provide no feedback on whether you could charge more. Many dentists interpret this silence as confirmation that their fees are appropriate, when in reality, they may be leaving significant revenue on the table year after year.

The problem compounds over time. If you've been in-network with a PPO for five to ten years without raising fees annually, the gap between your contracted rate and their actual maximum could exceed hundreds of thousands of dollars in lifetime revenue.

The Three-Tier Fee Structure

Think of dental fees as three distinct tiers:

  1. Your Usual and Customary (UCR) Fees: The full fee you'd charge to an uninsured patient. This is your ideal revenue target and represents your true professional value.
  2. Maximum Allowable Fees: The highest amount the insurance company will reimburse for each procedure, set internally but never disclosed to you.
  3. Your Current PPO Fees: The amount you've agreed to accept, often significantly below maximum allowable.

Your goal is to move tier three upward toward tier two, capturing the gap between what the insurance company will pay and what you're currently accepting.

Discovering Your Actual Fee Ceilings

Why Calling the Insurance Company Won't Help

Don't waste time calling Delta, United, or any other carrier asking for their maximum fee schedules. They will politely decline and tell you to submit your fee schedule for approval. They maintain this opacity intentionally—it's a business strategy.

The Professional Fee Analysis Approach

The most effective way to identify your fee schedule optimization opportunities is through a comprehensive fee analysis conducted by professionals who work across multiple insurance carriers and states. Companies like Veritas Dental Resources maintain databases of maximum allowable fees from all major PPO plans across all 50 states.

Key Insight

By analyzing your current fee schedule against their proprietary database, specialists can identify which specific procedures have room for increase, exactly how much you can safely raise each fee, and the specific maximum allowable for each insurance plan you're contracted with.

Why This Matters for Multi-Plan Practices

Many practices are contracted with six, eight, or even more PPO plans simultaneously. Each plan has a completely different fee schedule and maximum allowable structure. You cannot use a one-size-fits-all approach.

A professional fee analysis addresses this complexity by:

  • Analyzing each PPO plan individually
  • Identifying plan-specific procedures with optimization potential
  • Recommending customized fee schedules for each carrier
  • Prioritizing raises for procedures with the highest impact
  • Managing resubmission timing to avoid rejection

The Real Revenue Impact

From Writeoff to Net Profit

Here's the beautiful part: when you raise your PPO fees, every dollar goes directly to your bottom line. Your overhead doesn't increase. Your clinical time doesn't change. Your staff costs remain constant. You're simply capturing revenue that was previously left on the table.

If a fee analysis reveals that you can safely increase your average fees by 5-8%, and you're doing $500,000 in PPO production annually, that translates to $25,000-$40,000 in additional net income every single year.

Realistic Expectations

While fee optimization can be significant, it's important to maintain realistic expectations. If your practice currently has writeoffs of 45-50%, a strategic fee increase might reduce this to 40-45%—still meaningful progress that reduces the fee gap by approximately $50,000-$100,000 annually.

However, this represents a "first step" in reducing insurance dependence, not the complete solution. Maximum fee increases will close part of the gap between what you're currently accepting and what you could charge private-pay patients, but there will still be a meaningful difference.

Sequencing Your Strategy

Step One: Optimize Your Fees

While still in-network, work with a fee analysis company to identify and strategically raise your PPO fees to their maximum allowable. This captures immediate revenue with zero downside risk.

Step Two: Build Marketing Infrastructure

Before resigning from PPO plans, establish a robust digital marketing strategy to replace the patient flow that currently comes from insurance networks. Expect to lose 10-15% of your PPO patient base when you go out-of-network, plus all the new PPO patients you would have acquired.

This means you need to actively build and optimize your digital marketing presence—SEO, content, local listings, and patient reviews—minimum six to twelve months before PPO resignation.

Step Three: Reduce Insurance Dependence Systematically

Once your fees are optimized and your marketing is established, you can confidently move forward with PPO resignation, knowing you have a plan to replace lost patient flow with ideal private-pay patients.

Taking Action

Your first action item is straightforward: understand your actual insurance writeoffs. How much are you currently accepting in discounts across your various PPO plans? Many practices are shocked to discover they're writing off $400,000-$600,000 annually.

Once you understand the magnitude of the gap, connecting with a professional fee analysis service makes immediate sense. The investment in a comprehensive analysis typically pays for itself within the first month through identified fee optimization opportunities.

Key Takeaway: You cannot negotiate your fees upward after signing a PPO agreement. But you can strategically raise your fees within the agreement's parameters by identifying procedures where you're below the insurance company's maximum allowable. This is one of the few legitimate ways to capture additional revenue while still in-network.
