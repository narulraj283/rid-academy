Three Ways Your Patients Benefit When You Drop PPO Plans

8 min read Updated March 2025

The dental insurance system primarily benefits one party: the insurance company. Dropping PPO plans isn't just advantageous for your practice—it directly benefits your patients through fairer pricing, comprehensive coverage without limitations, and the time your team has to truly care for their oral health.

Understanding the Four-Party System

Many dentists don't realize that the dental insurance landscape involves four distinct entities: the insurance company, the dentist, the patient, and the employer who purchased the policy. In today's PPO-dominated system, only one party consistently wins: the insurance company.

The dentist loses due to 42-44% fee discounts imposed by PPO networks. Patients lose due to complex coverage rules, waiting periods, and claim denials. Employers lose because insurance companies charge premiums 50-70% higher than they should be. The insurance company maintains maximum profitability while the other three parties absorb the costs.

Three Ways Patients Benefit When You Go Out of Network

1. Fairer Fees for All Patients

Consider a real scenario: In a PPO practice, a crown typically costs $1,700 on the fee schedule. The contracted PPO fee is $800. This means:

  • Insured patients pay $800
  • Uninsured patients pay $1,700
  • The uninsured patients are subsidizing the insured patients

This system is inherently unfair. When you drop PPO plans and establish a reasonable fee structure—say $1,300 for everyone—all patients pay the same transparent price. This creates ethical equity: no patient is subsidizing another's care.

An inclusive fee structure benefits all patients by eliminating hidden subsidization and creating transparent pricing that everyone can trust.

2. Comprehensive Coverage Through In-Office Membership Plans

Many practices transitioning away from PPO plans implement in-office membership programs that offer patients a 10% savings on all procedures with no limitations or games. This approach provides several advantages:

  • Patients save on every procedure—cleanings, fillings, crowns, and complex cases
  • No waiting periods to meet before coverage begins
  • No claim denials for timing issues (like being 5 months and 29 days instead of 6 months between cleanings)
  • Full mouth reconstruction cases cost thousands less
  • Predictable annual benefits patients can plan around

Unlike PPO plans with their exclusions, waiting periods, and fine-print restrictions, membership plans are straightforward and benefit-focused. A patient requiring $40,000 in treatment saves $4,000 immediately—without playing insurance games.

3. Access to Time-Centered Care and Prevention

The most profound benefit many patients overlook is access to preventive, relationship-based care. When you drop PPO plans, your team no longer operates on the "hamster wheel"—frantically seeing double the patients to compensate for insurance discounts.

This time investment allows dentists to:

  • Develop genuine relationships with patients
  • Ask detailed health history questions
  • Diagnose conditions early, potentially saving lives
  • Connect oral health to overall systemic health
  • Implement true wellness models instead of "fix-it" models

The Life-Saving Impact of Time

Consider the power of preventive dentistry when a provider has adequate time. Dentists can screen for obstructive sleep apnea—affecting millions of Americans—or notice early signs of oral cancer. A provider who knows their patient's complete health history can identify patterns others might miss. This isn't just about teeth; it's about overall health.

The contrast is striking between the "fix-it" model of PPO dentistry and the "wellness" model that emerges when insurance doesn't dictate every interaction. Insurance companies won't pay for a dentist to spend time understanding a patient's overall health, asking preventive questions, or exploring root causes of problems. They want problems coded and fixed efficiently.

Beyond Insurance: A Patient-Centered Approach

When patients transition from insurance-dependent practices to out-of-network providers, they often report feeling genuinely cared for. They're not treated as claim codes to be processed. They're patients whose health matters.

This shift affects three dimensions of patient satisfaction:

  • Financial Transparency: Clear, fair pricing without hidden subsidies
  • Coverage Security: Predictable benefits with no surprise denials
  • Care Quality: Time with their dentist to address their unique needs

The Ripple Effect: Better Patients, Better Outcomes

When patients benefit from fairer fees, comprehensive plans, and quality time with their dentist, they become more engaged in their oral health. They're more likely to follow preventive recommendations, attend regular appointments, and pursue comprehensive treatment plans because cost barriers are minimized and trust is maximized.

This creates a virtuous cycle: healthier patients, stronger practice relationships, and a team that practices dentistry the way they were trained to—focused on patient health rather than insurance codes.
