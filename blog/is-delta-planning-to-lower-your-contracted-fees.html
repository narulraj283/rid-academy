Is Delta Planning to Lower Your Contracted Fees?

3 min read

Delta Dental's fee schedule changes have sent shockwaves through the dental community. Understanding what's happening and how to protect your practice is critical.

The Delta Fee Schedule Landscape

Over the past several years, Delta Dental has implemented significant changes to their contracted fee schedules in many states. For some providers, this has meant stagnant or even reduced reimbursements at a time when overhead costs continue to rise. Understanding these trends is essential for making informed decisions about your PPO participation.

What the Data Shows

Many dentists have reported receiving notifications of fee schedule adjustments that effectively reduce their compensation. When you factor in inflation and rising costs of supplies, equipment, and labor, even fees that remain flat represent a real decrease in purchasing power. The gap between what it costs to deliver quality care and what PPOs reimburse continues to widen.

How This Affects Your Practice

Lower contracted fees create a cascading effect. To maintain revenue, practices feel pressured to increase patient volume, which can compromise quality and lead to burnout. It creates a treadmill effect where you work harder to earn less. This is precisely why many practices are evaluating their Delta participation.

Strategic Options

You have several paths forward. First, analyze your Delta patient data to understand the true revenue impact. Second, consider whether a phased approach to reducing Delta dependence makes sense. Third, invest in building systems that attract patients who value quality over insurance coverage. The practices that thrive are those that proactively plan rather than react.
