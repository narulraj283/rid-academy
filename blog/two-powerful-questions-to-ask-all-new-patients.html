PATIENT COMMUNICATION

Two Powerful Questions to Ask All New Patients

3 min read

Asking the right questions during a new patient visit can transform your case acceptance rate and set the foundation for a fee-for-service relationship.

Why the Right Questions Matter

Most dental practices focus on clinical questions during the initial exam. While important, these miss the opportunity to understand what truly motivates patients. When you understand their deeper concerns, you can present treatment in a way that resonates with their values rather than their insurance coverage. Practices that master this skill consistently see higher case acceptance and stronger patient loyalty.

Question One: What Brought You to Our Practice Today?

This open-ended question accomplishes several things at once. It lets patients share their story, reveals their primary concern, and helps you understand their expectations. The key is to listen actively without interrupting. Patients who feel heard are significantly more likely to accept comprehensive treatment plans regardless of insurance coverage. Document their answer prominently in their record.

Question Two: If You Could Change Anything About Your Smile, What Would It Be?

This question shifts the conversation from problem-focused to aspiration-focused. It invites patients to dream about possibilities rather than just fixing what hurts. When patients articulate their ideal outcome, they become emotionally invested in achieving it. This emotional connection is far more powerful than any insurance benefit in driving treatment acceptance.

Implementing These Questions

Train every team member to ask these questions naturally during the intake process. During treatment presentations, connect your recommendations back to what the patient told you they wanted. This approach builds trust and demonstrates that you listen and care about their individual needs, which is the cornerstone of a successful fee-for-service practice.
