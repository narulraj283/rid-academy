How to Successfully Overcome Team Resistance to Dropping PPOs

3 min read

Your team's buy-in is essential when transitioning away from PPO dependence. Here's how to bring them along on the journey.

Understanding Why Teams Resist

Fear is the primary driver of resistance. Team members worry about losing patients, losing their jobs, and dealing with upset patients. These fears are natural and valid. Dismissing them will only increase resistance. Instead, address them head-on with data, education, and a clear plan.

Sharing the Vision and the Numbers

Show your team the financial reality of PPO participation. When they see how much revenue is written off annually and how that money could fund raises, bonuses, better equipment, and reduced stress, the motivation shifts. Help them understand that fewer patients at full fees often means a better work environment for everyone.

Role-Playing Patient Conversations

The biggest anxiety for team members is explaining the change to patients. Invest heavily in role-playing exercises where team members practice having these conversations. Provide scripts that feel natural and empathetic. When your team feels confident in their ability to communicate the change, resistance melts away.

Celebrating Wins Along the Way

As you begin dropping PPOs, celebrate every positive outcome. Share stories of patients who stayed despite the change. Highlight the financial improvements. When team members see real results matching what you promised, they become your strongest advocates for the transition.
